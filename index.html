The Golden Hour

58 HOURS OIL ON WOOD

The Spark: This started as a sketch of a wilting sunflower in late September. It represents the beauty of things coming to an end. The Labor: To get the specific glow of the light, I applied 14 layers of transparent yellow glaze, waiting 48 hours for each layer to dry before the next. The Truth: Some people see a flower; I see the two weeks I spent trying to figure out why the shadows felt "heavy" instead of "warm."

Resilience in Blue

72 HOURS MIXED MEDIA

The Spark: Created during a period of intense personal change. The abstract movements reflect the chaos of making big life decisions. The Labor: The texture was created by mixing sand from a local beach into the paint. It was physically exhausting to move the heavy medium across the canvas. The Truth: The blue is not just a color choice—it's the exact shade of the ocean on the day I realized I needed to start over.

The Architect's Dream

110 HOURS FINE LINER & ACRYLIC

The Spark: An exploration of how our memories of places differ from the actual locations. It’s a "dreamscape" of my childhood home. The Labor: Every single gold line was drawn with a 0.05mm pen. If my hand slipped once, the entire geometric balance would be ruined. The Truth: I wore a wrist brace for three days after finishing this. People ask why it’s expensive—they are paying for the precision of my focus.
